Hello! I am <span style="color:#2a7ae2;"><strong>Muhammad Suffian</strong></span>, a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the AI-Lab of the <span style="color:#2a7ae2;"><strong>Mediterranea University of Reggio Calabria</strong></span>. My research focuses on <span style="color:#d14;"><strong>Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)</strong></span>, with an emphasis on developing transparent and human-centred AI models for <span style="color:#0a7f5a;"><strong>health and environmental monitoring</strong></span>.

Currently, I work on designing explainable deep learning approaches for rehabilitation of brain-disorder patients using <span style="color:#d14;"><strong>EEG signals</strong></span>, leveraging advanced architectures such as <strong>3D-CNNs</strong>, <strong>Vision Transformers</strong>, and hybrid interpretable models. This work is carried out in collaboration with <span style="color:#2a7ae2;"><strong>g.tec engineering GmbH</strong></span> on real-world data.

I hold a PhD from the <span style="color:#2a7ae2;"><strong>University of Urbino Carlo Bo</strong></span>, where my research focused on <span style="color:#d14;"><strong>human-centred counterfactual explanations</strong></span> in AI. My academic journey also includes research experience at <strong>CTESquare</strong> and as a Visiting Researcher at <span style="color:#2a7ae2;"><strong>CiTIUS</strong></span>, where I explored advanced XAI methodologies.

I am particularly interested in building interpretable AI systems that integrate <span style="color:#d14;"><strong>human-in-the-loop approaches</strong></span> and contribute to impactful, real-world applications.
